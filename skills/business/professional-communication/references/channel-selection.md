# Channel Selection Matrix

Detailed guidance for choosing the right communication channel based on
urgency, complexity, audience, and desired outcome.

## Decision Criteria

When selecting a channel, consider:

1. **Urgency** — How quickly does the recipient need to act?
2. **Complexity** — How much context or nuance is required?
3. **Audience** — How many people, and what roles?
4. **Record needed?** — Does this need a documented trail?
5. **Interactivity** — Does this require back-and-forth discussion?

## Channel Matrix

| Channel | Urgency | Complexity | Audience | Record | Interactivity |
|---------|---------|------------|----------|--------|---------------|
| Email | Low–Medium | Low–High | 1–many | Yes | Low |
| Instant Messaging | Medium–High | Low–Medium | 1–few | Partial | Medium |
| Text Message | Very High | Low | 1 | No | Low |
| Phone Call | Medium–High | Medium–High | 1–2 | No (summarize after) | High |
| Meeting | Scheduled | High | 2–many | Agenda + notes | High |
| Document / Wiki | Low | High | many | Yes | Low |

## Detailed Channel Guidance

### Email

- **Use for:** Formal announcements, detailed project updates, documenting
  conversations, and any non-urgent communication that requires a record.
- **Best practice:** Email is the default for most professional communication.
  It is asynchronous and respects the recipient's focus time.
- **When not to use:** Long back-and-forth, unresolved debate, or high emotion.
  Use a call or chat instead, then send a short email summary with the decision.

### Instant Messaging (Slack, Teams, etc.)

- **Use for:** Quick questions, informal collaboration, and time-sensitive
  coordination.
- **Best practice:** Be mindful that instant messages can be disruptive. For
  complex topics, send a brief message suggesting a follow-up conversation or
  email rather than dumping a wall of text into chat.
- **When not to use:** Formal announcements, anything requiring a permanent
  record, or sensitive personnel matters.

### Text Messages

- **Use for:** Extremely urgent, time-sensitive matters when other channels are
  unavailable (e.g., "The building's fire alarm is going off," or "I'm running
  15 minutes late for our off-site meeting").
- **Best practice:** Avoid using text messages for any non-urgent business
  communication. Reserve for genuine emergencies.

### Phone Calls (Business Phone & Cell)

- **Use for:** Discussing complex or sensitive topics that require nuanced,
  real-time conversation but don't need a formal meeting. Ideal for resolving
  misunderstandings quickly.
- **Best practice:** Respect colleagues' time by sending a brief message first
  (e.g., "Do you have 5 minutes for a quick call about X?") rather than calling
  unannounced. Follow up with an email summarizing key decisions and action
  items.

### Meetings (In-Person or Video Conference)

- **Use for:** Collaborative work sessions, strategic planning, decision-making
  involving multiple stakeholders, and important team announcements where
  discussion is necessary.
- **Best practice:** Schedule a meeting only when the objective cannot be
  achieved asynchronously. Always send an invitation with a clear agenda,
  desired outcomes, and any necessary pre-reading materials.

### Documents / Wikis

- **Use for:** Reference material, policies, detailed plans, and anything that
  will be consulted repeatedly over time.
- **Best practice:** Link to the document in a short email or chat message
  rather than pasting long content into a message. Keep documents
  version-controlled and clearly owned.

## Escalation Path

When a channel isn't working, escalate appropriately:

1. **No email reply after 2 business days** → send a brief chat message
2. **Chat goes unresolved after a few exchanges** → propose a quick call
3. **Call doesn't resolve the issue** → schedule a meeting with stakeholders
4. **Meeting decision needs documentation** → send an email summary with action
   items and owners
