# Message Templates

Templates for common internal communication scenarios. Adapt the tone and
detail to your audience and context.

## Project Update Email

**Subject:** UPDATE: [Project Name] — [Status as of Date]

```
Hi team,

[Project Name] is on track for [delivery date]. Key updates:

- [Milestone 1]: [status]
- [Milestone 2]: [status]
- [Risk or blocker]: [description and mitigation]

Next steps:
- [Action item] — Owner: [name], Due: [date]
- [Action item] — Owner: [name], Due: [date]

Questions? Reply here or grab me on chat.
```

## Action Request Email

**Subject:** ACTION REQUIRED: [What you need] by [deadline]

```
Hi [name],

I need [specific action] from you by [deadline].

Context: [1–2 sentences of necessary background]

What I need from you:
- [Specific deliverable or decision]

If you foresee any issues meeting this deadline, let me know by [earlier date]
so we can adjust.
```

## Decision Email

**Subject:** DECISION: [Topic] — [chosen option]

```
Hi [stakeholders],

We've decided to [chosen option] for [topic].

Rationale:
- [Reason 1]
- [Reason 2]

Alternatives considered:
- [Option B]: [why not chosen]
- [Option C]: [why not chosen]

Next steps:
- [Implementation action] — Owner: [name], Due: [date]

Reply if you have concerns by [deadline]; otherwise we proceed as planned.
```

## Risk Notification Email

**Subject:** RISK: [Risk description] — [impact level]

```
Hi [stakeholders],

[Describe the risk in one sentence]. Impact: [what could go wrong].

Current status: [what's happening now]

Mitigation plan:
- [Step 1] — Owner: [name], Due: [date]
- [Step 2] — Owner: [name], Due: [date]

I'll send updates as the situation evolves. Questions? Reply here.
```

## Acknowledging an Employee Complaint

**Subject:** Re: [Original subject]

```
Hi [name],

Thank you for bringing this to our attention. We take these matters seriously
and will review the information in accordance with company policy.

Next steps:
- [What happens next, e.g., "HR will review and follow up within X business days"]
- [Any immediate action, if applicable]

If you have additional information to share, please [instructions].

[Neutral, professional closing]
```

> **Note:** Avoid making admissions or judgments before a review is complete.
> Keep the tone professional and neutral. Focus on process and next steps.

## Formal Feedback (PIP) Communication

**Subject:** Performance Improvement Plan — [Employee name]

```
Hi [name],

This document outlines specific, measurable performance objectives for the next
[timeframe, e.g., 60 days] and the resources we will provide to support you in
meeting them.

Objectives:
- [Objective 1]: [measurable target] by [date]
- [Objective 2]: [measurable target] by [date]

Support provided:
- [Resource 1, e.g., weekly 1:1 coaching]
- [Resource 2, e.g., training access]

Review checkpoints:
- [Date 1]: [what will be reviewed]
- [Date 2]: [what will be reviewed]

Please review and acknowledge receipt by signing below.
```

> **Note:** Tone must be strictly factual, non-emotional, and focused on
> observable behaviors and measurable outcomes. Structure around evidence
> (dates, metrics, specific examples). Ensure the employee's acknowledgement
> of receipt is documented.

## Giving Constructive Feedback

```
Hi [name],

I appreciated [specific positive observation about their work].

One area where I see room for improvement: [specific, observable behavior].
The impact is [why it matters].

Would you be open to [suggested approach or conversation]? I'm happy to support
however I can.
```
