# Sourcing Guide by Product Category

> **In-person vs. online**: Most channels below are online. Where a channel is
> in-person only or offers in-person pickup/inspection, it's noted in the
> Notes column. When the user specifies a travel radius, filter for in-person
> channels within that distance and online channels everywhere. Auction
> platforms that require local pickup (GovDeals, Public Surplus, USPS) are
> online for bidding but in-person for collection — factor travel cost into
> the true price.

## Electronics & Computers

| Channel | When to use | Typical savings | Notes |
|---------|------------|-----------------|-------|
| Manufacturer refurbished | Always check first | 15–40% | Often includes full warranty |
| [Back Market](https://www.backmarket.com/) | Phones, laptops, tablets | 20–50% | Graded condition system |
| [Swappa](https://swappa.com/) | Phones, laptops, watches | 15–35% | Verified devices, no junk |
| Amazon Warehouse | Open-box / returned | 10–30% | "Used - Like New" is often pristine |
| Best Buy Open Box | In-store only | 10–25% | Negotiable at store level |
| B-stock / liquidation | Bulk or high risk tolerance | 30–60% | [B-Stock](https://bstock.com/), [Liquidation.com](https://www.liquidation.com/) |
| eBay (certified refurbished) | When warranty matters | 15–30% | Allstate 2-year warranty included |
| [PropertyRoom](https://www.propertyroom.com/) | Seized electronics, jewelry | 30–70% | Police auctions; 16.5% buyer's premium; most start at $1 no reserve |
| GovDeals electronics lots | Bulk IT equipment, surplus | 20–50% | State/local schools surplus IT; 7.5–12.5% buyer's premium |
| USPS unclaimed mail lots | Mystery lots, gamble | Variable | [GovDeals Atlanta Surplus Center](https://www.govdeals.com/) — sold in bulk lots, pickup only |

**Never buy used**: Batteries below 80% health, items with water damage indicators tripped, counterfeit-prone items (cables, chargers, memory cards).

| [Copart](https://www.copart.com/) / [IAAI](https://www.iaai.com/) (repo section) | Bank/credit union repos sent to auction | 30–60% | Also lists impound vehicles; title issues; dealer license may be required for some sales |
| [BidNDrive](https://www.bidndrive.com/) | Repossessed cars, impounded vehicles | 30–50% | Online repo auctions; pre-bid and live bidding |
| City impound auctions | Abandoned/seized vehicles | 20–50% | Search `"<city> impound auction"` — e.g., [NYC DOF](https://www.nyc.gov/site/finance/vehicles/auctions.page), [Long Beach](https://www.longbeach.gov/finance/services-and-permits/towing-and-lien-sales/auction/); in-person or online depending on city |

**Mileage thresholds to avoid**:
- CVT transmissions: avoid 100k+ miles
- Turbo engines: avoid 80k+ without maintenance records
- Diesel particulate filters: avoid 150k+ without regen history
- Timing belt engines: buy only if belt was replaced at interval or budget $800–$1,500 for it

## Government Auctions & Surplus — Federal

The federal government auctions seized/forfeited property and surplus through
agency-run sites and contracted auctioneers. Start at the
[USA.gov auctions portal](https://www.usa.gov/auctions-and-sales) for a
cross-agency index.

### Federal Agency Auction Sites

| Agency | What they sell | Site / Portal | Buyer's premium |
|--------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| [GSA Auctions](https://gsaauctions.gov/) | Federal surplus — vehicles, equipment, real property, everything | gsaauctions.gov | **None** |
| [GSA Real Estate Sales](https://realestatesales.gov/) | Surplus federal buildings, land, facilities | realestatesales.gov | None (reserve sales) |
| [US Marshals Service](https://www.usmarshals.gov/what-we-do/asset-forfeiture) | Seized/forfeited: real estate, vehicles, vessels, aircraft, jewelry, art, crypto, domain names | usmarshals.gov → contracted auctioneers (below) | Varies by contractor |
| [Treasury / IRS](https://home.treasury.gov/services/treasury-auctions) | Tax-forfeiture property — real estate, vehicles, merchandise | [treasury.gov/auctions/treasury/rp/](https://www.treasury.gov/auctions/treasury/rp/) (real property), [treasury.gov/auctions/treasury/gp/](https://www.treasury.gov/auctions/treasury/gp/) (general) | Varies |
| [HUD HomeStore](https://www.hudhomestore.gov/) | FHA foreclosed single-family homes, Dollar Homes initiative | hudhomestore.gov | None (must use HUD-registered agent) |

## Government Auctions — State & Local

Most state and local governments contract out their surplus auctions to
private platforms rather than running their own. The three dominant platforms
are GovDeals, Public Surplus, and Municibid. Some states also run their own
surplus stores and retail locations.

### Government Auction Contractor Platforms

| Platform | Government level | What they sell | Buyer's premium | Notes |
|----------|----------------|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| [GovDeals](https://www.govdeals.com/) | State, local, some federal (USPS) | Vehicles, equipment, IT, furniture, confiscated property — largest inventory (15k–25k active listings) | 7.5–12.5% (tiered) | Owned by Liquidity Services (NASDAQ: LQDT); 5,300+ government clients; mobile app |
| [Public Surplus](https://www.publicsurplus.com/) | State, local, universities, school districts | Similar to GovDeals — smaller inventory (5k–10k) | 0–10% (set per seller, median ~7%) | No mobile app; simpler fee structure |

| [PropertyRoom](https://www.propertyroom.com/) | 4,300+ law enforcement agencies | Police-seized, stolen, abandoned, unclaimed goods — jewelry, electronics, vehicles, watches | 16.5% | Largest online police auction site; most start at $1 no reserve |
| [Bid4Assets](https://www.bid4assets.com/) | Federal (US Marshals, Treasury), county tax sales | Real estate, forfeited property, tax liens | Varies | Also runs county tax deed sales |

GovDeals or Public Surplus with a state-specific storefront.

### Local / Municipal / Police Auctions

City and county governments, police departments, school districts, and
transit authorities all auction surplus and seized property. Most use the
contractor platforms above (GovDeals, Public Surplus, PropertyRoom), but some
run their own.

| Type | How to find | Examples |
|------|------------|---------|
| City/county surplus | Search `"<city/county> surplus auction"` or check GovDeals/Public Surplus | [LA County](https://lacounty.gov/business/doing-business-with-la-county/auctions-and-surplus-property/), [Denver](https://www.denvergov.org/Government/Agencies-Departments-Offices/Agencies-Departments-Offices-Directory/General-Services/Surplus-Auctions), [Port Arthur TX](https://www.portarthurtx.gov/193/Auction-Surplus-Items) |
| Police seized property | Most use [PropertyRoom](https://www.propertyroom.com/) or GovDeals | [Maryland State Police](https://mdsp.maryland.gov/Pages/MSPPropertyAuctions.aspx), [San Antonio SAPD](https://www.sa.gov/Directory/Departments/SAPD/Services/Seized-Property-Auctions), [Temple Terrace FL](https://www.templeterrace.gov/337/Auction-Information), [Kingsport TN](https://www.kingsporttn.gov/city-services/police-department/administrative_bureau/other_administrative_units/seized-surplus-property-auctions/) |
| County tax deed/lien sales | Search `"<county> tax deed auction"` — many via [Bid4Assets](https://www.bid4assets.com/) | County courthouse steps or online; varies by state |
| School district surplus | GovDeals / Public Surplus — search by school district name | IT equipment, buses, furniture, kitchen equipment |
| Transit authority | Search `"<transit agency> surplus auction"` | Buses, vans, parts, equipment |
| Airport lost & found | Some airports auction via GovDeals; Texas state surplus store sells unclaimed airport property | Check individual airport websites |

**In-person auctions**: Many county tax deed sales and some police auctions
still happen in person at courthouses or police parking lots. Search
`"<county> tax deed sale"` or `"<city> police auction in person"`. These
typically have less competition than online auctions but require showing up
in person with a cashier's check.

## Community & Neighborhood Marketplaces

Hyper-local buying, selling, and free-gifting platforms. Best for furniture,
household goods, appliances, kids' items, and anything where shipping costs
would kill the deal.

### Free-Gifting Platforms

| Platform | Best for | Reach | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|-------|
| [Buy Nothing Project](https://buynothingproject.org/) | Hyperlocal free gifting | Global, neighborhood-level | App + Facebook groups; ultra-local gifting circles; community-focused |

**Strategy**: Post a "Wanted" ad on Freecycle/Trash Nothing for specific items
(furniture, appliances, moving boxes). Many people have items they'd give away
but haven't listed. Join your local Buy Nothing group on Facebook or the app
for the most active hyper-local free gifting.

## Thrift Stores — Online & In-Person

Thrift stores run by charities and nonprofits sell donated goods at a fraction
of retail. Many now have online storefronts in addition to their brick-and-
mortar locations. Best for clothing, books, furniture, housewares, and
one-of-a-kind vintage/collectible finds.

Also eBay and Amazon storefronts per region (e.g., [SF Goodwill on eBay](https://stores.ebay.com/goodwillsanfrancisco)) | ShopGoodwill is the main online thrift auction site; new items daily; 5-day auctions; authenticated designer items |

### Online Consignment & Secondhand Marketplaces

| Platform | Best for | Typical savings | Notes |
|----------|----------|-----------------|-------|
| [ThredUp](https://www.thredup.com/) | Women's & kids' clothing, curated | 50–90% off retail | Professionally photographed; quality-checked; 60k new arrivals/day; easiest hands-off thrift experience; returns accepted |
| [Poshmark](https://poshmark.com/) | Designer & contemporary brands, fashion | 50–80% off retail | Social marketplace; offer system for negotiation; authentication for luxury items over $500; $7.67 flat-rate shipping |
| [Depop](https://www.depop.com/) | Vintage, Y2K, streetwear, Gen Z style | 40–80% off retail | Instagram-like interface; best for unique/vintage clothing; quality varies by seller |
| [Mercari](https://www.mercari.com/) | Everything — electronics, toys, clothing, home goods | 30–70% off retail | Not fashion-focused; lowest prices due to less competition; flexible shipping; 10% seller fee |
| [ThriftBooks](https://www.thriftbooks.com/) | Used books — 13M+ titles | 50–90% off retail | Free shipping over $20; graded condition; out-of-print and rare titles; Wish List notifications |
| [BetterWorldBooks](https://www.betterworldbooks.com/) | Used books | 50–90% off retail | Carbon-neutral shipping; funds literacy programs worldwide |
| [eBay](https://www.ebay.com/) (secondhand) | Everything | 20–70% off retail | Largest secondhand marketplace; check sold listings for price benchmarks; buyer protection |

**Strategy**: For clothing, start with ThredUp for curated basics, then check
Poshmark for specific brands and Depop for vintage. For books, ThriftBooks
and BetterWorldBooks are cheaper than Amazon for used titles. For general
thrift treasure hunting (jewelry, art, collectibles, electronics),
ShopGoodwill.com is the best online thrift auction site — new items listed
daily, most starting at $1.

**In-person thrift tip**: Most thrift stores restock daily and have weekly
discount days (senior, student, military) and color-tag sales (items with a
certain color tag are 50% off that week). Ask the cashier about the discount
schedule. Goodwill typically runs 50% off on Saturdays for a specific color
tag; Savers has 50% off on Mondays for club members.

## Corporate Liquidation & Business Surplus

When companies close, downsize, swap equipment, refresh IT, or liquidate
excess inventory, they sell through B2B liquidation platforms and asset
disposition firms — not eBay. These channels sell in bulk (pallets,
truckloads, lots) at 10–40% of retail, making them ideal for resellers,
small businesses, or buyers willing to take a pallet and sort/split it.

### Retail Liquidation Platforms (Pallets & Truckloads)

These platforms sell customer returns, overstock, and shelf pulls from major
retailers like Amazon, Walmart, Target, Home Depot, and Costco. Inventory
comes in pallets or truckloads — you buy blind or with a manifest.

| Platform | What they sell | How it works | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------------|-------|
| [B-Stock](https://bstock.com/) | Returns & excess from Amazon, Walmart, Target, Home Depot, Best Buy, Wayfair, and 500+ retailers | Auction-style; per-retailer storefronts (e.g., Amazon Liquidations, Walmart Liquidations) | Largest B2B liquidation marketplace; $15B+ in transactions; buyer's premium varies by seller; must register as a reseller |
| [Liquidation.com](https://www.liquidation.com/) | Overstock, returns, surplus from Home Depot, Sony, Staples, government surplus | Auction + fixed price; warehouses nationwide for pickup | One of the oldest liquidation sites; lot sizes from single pallet to truckload; shipping available |
| [Direct Liquidation](https://www.directliquidation.com/) | Brand-name returns, overstock, closeouts | Auction + fixed price; condition grades | Best for Amazon FBA sellers; manifested lots; ships from Lenexa, KS |
| [BULQ](https://www.bulq.com/) | Returns & overstock from major retailers | Fixed price (no bidding); condition grades (new, like new, uninspected, salvage) | Run by Optoro; easiest for beginners; no returns/exchanges; buys in 30+ categories |
| [BoxFox](https://www.boxfox.com/) | Overstock & returns from department stores, big-box retailers | Auction-style; manifested lots | Smaller platform; good for smaller resellers; per-lot bidding |
| [888Lots](https://www.888lots.com/) | Overstock, returns, closeout merchandise | Fixed price per lot; manifested | Good for eBay/Amazon sellers; per-item manifests available |
| [Via Trading](https://www.viatrading.com/) | Customer returns, overstock, closeouts | Fixed price; in-person warehouse shopping (CA, NV) | Good for West Coast buyers; minimum order $300; warehouse pickup |

| [Select Liquidation](https://selectliquidation.com/) | Pallets & truckloads from major retailers | Auction + fixed price | Newer platform; good selection of home goods and electronics |

### Enterprise IT Asset Disposition (ITAD)

When companies refresh laptops, servers, networking gear, or decommission
data centers, they use ITAD firms that wipe data and resell the equipment.
Some sell directly to the public; others sell through B-Stock, GovDeals, or
their own auction sites.

| Firm | What they handle | How to buy |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| [Liquis](https://liquis.com/) | Full facility liquidation, data center decommissioning, office furniture, IT equipment | Contact for auction schedules; handles Fortune 500 corporate liquidations |
| [Surplus Liquidation Solutions (SLS)](https://surplusliquidationsolutions.com/) | Enterprise IT, capital equipment, surplus inventory | Direct purchase + auction; Fortune 1000 clients; contact for inventory lists |
| [TAMS Solutions](https://tamsolutions.com/) | IT asset recovery, data center decommissioning | Contact for resale inventory; NIST 800-88 data destruction certified |
| [C2 Management](https://c2management.com/) | Data center ITAD, GPU/AI clusters, medical, industrial | R2v3 & ISO certified; contact for value recovery inventory |

### How to Find Corporate Liquidations

| Method | How |
|--------|-----|
| Search for bankruptcy auctions | Search PACER court records or `"<company> bankruptcy auction"` — bankruptcy trustees auction assets |
| Search for closing business sales | Craigslist "business" section, FB Marketplace, local newspaper classifieds — `"business liquidation"`, `"store closing sale"`, `"going out of business"` |
| Contact ITAD firms directly | Call SLS, Liquis, TAMS, C2 — ask about available inventory or auction schedules |
| Watch B-Stock per-retailer storefronts | Create alerts on B-Stock for specific retailers (Amazon, Walmart, Target) and categories |
| Check AllSurplus and Salvex | Browse industrial categories — equipment, materials, inventory lots |
| Search HiBid and AuctionZip | Filter by "business liquidation", "industrial", "equipment" categories |
| Local auction houses | Search `"<city> auction house"` — many handle business liquidations and corporate asset sales |

**Strategy**: For resellers, B-Stock is the primary source for retail returns
and overstock. For IT equipment, watch GovDeals (school/government IT
surplus) and contact ITAD firms directly for corporate refresh cycles. For
industrial equipment, Ritchie Bros. and AllSurplus are the main channels.
For one-off business closures, check local auction houses and Craigslist/FB
Marketplace "business liquidation" listings.

**Risks**: Liquidation pallets are sold as-is with no returns. Manifests may
be inaccurate. Customer returns have higher defect rates than overstock.
Condition grades vary by platform — "uninspected" means you're gambling.
Start with a small pallet from BULQ (fixed price, no bidding) before moving
to B-Stock auctions.

## Repossession, Tow Yards & Impound Auctions

Vehicles that have been repossessed by lenders (banks, credit unions) or
abandoned/impounded by tow companies and cities are sold at a fraction of
dealer prices. These are often overlooked sourcing channels.

### Repossessed Vehicles (Bank/Credit Union)

When a borrower defaults on an auto loan, the lender repossesses the vehicle
and sells it to recover the loan balance. These are often sold below market
because the lender wants quick liquidation.

| Channel | What they sell | Site / How to find | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| [RepoFinder.com](https://www.repofinder.com/) | Aggregates bank/credit union repo listings | repofinder.com | Direct-from-lender; no buyer's premium; inventory changes often |
| Credit union websites | Repossessed cars, boats, RVs, motorcycles | Check local CU websites — e.g., [America First CU](https://repos.americafirst.com/), [Golden West CU](https://cars.gwcu.org/), [WEOKIE FCU](https://www.weokie.org/auto/repo-vehicles), [Eglin FCU](https://www.eglinfcu.org/repos/) | Sold as-is; some CUs offer financing; open to public (not just members) |
| [Copart](https://www.copart.com/) (repo section) | Lender-sent repos at auction | copart.com — filter by sale type | Dealer license may be required for some sales; title brands vary |
| [IAAI](https://www.iaai.com/) (repo section) | Lender-sent repos at auction | iaai.com — filter by sale type | Similar to Copart; insurance and repo inventory mixed |
| [BidNDrive](https://www.bidndrive.com/) | Online repo auctions | bidndrive.com | Pre-bid and live bidding; repossessed and impounded vehicles |
| [Abetter.bid](https://abetter.bid/) | Repossessed cars for sale | abetter.bid | State-filtered; repo sale type |

**Strategy**: Call local credit unions and banks directly — ask for the
repo/loss mitigation department. Many sell directly to the public without
listing online, and smaller institutions may not even have a web page for
their repo inventory. This is the lowest-competition channel for repossessed
vehicles.

### Tow Yard / Impound Auctions

When vehicles are towed and not claimed by the owner within the statutory
hold period (typically 30–90 days depending on state), the tow company or
city impound lot sells them via lien sale auctions.

| Channel | What they sell | Site / How to find | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| [Autura Marketplace](https://marketplace.autura.com/) | Abandoned vehicles from tow/impound lots | marketplace.autura.com | Online bidding; thousands of listings; mobile app |
| [TOWLOT.com](https://www.towlot.com/) | Tow yard lien sale auctions | towlot.com | Online; tow operators list unclaimed vehicles |
| [OAI Auctions](https://oaikc.com/) | City impound auctions (e.g., Kansas City) | oaikc.com | In-person viewing on scheduled days; online bidding |
| City impound lots | Abandoned/seized city vehicles | Search `"<city> impound auction"` — e.g., [NYC DOF](https://www.nyc.gov/site/finance/vehicles/auctions.page), [Long Beach Towing](https://www.longbeach.gov/finance/services-and-permits/towing-and-lien-sales/auction/) | In-person or online; cash/cashier's check; no warranty |
| Private tow yards | Lien sale vehicles | Search `"<city> tow yard auction"` or `"<city> lien sale vehicle"` | Call local tow companies directly; many run their own monthly auctions |

**Risks**: Title may be salvage or abandoned-property bill of sale (not a
clean title). Vehicles are sold as-is with no inspection or test drive.
Mechanical condition is unknown — budget for repairs. Check your state's
DMV requirements for abandoned vehicle titles before bidding.

## Storage Unit Auctions

When a storage unit tenant stops paying rent, the facility auctions the
unit's contents after a statutory lien notice period. Contents are a gamble
— you buy everything in the unit sight-unseen (photos only).

| Platform | What it is | Site |
|----------|-----------|------|
| [StorageTreasures](https://www.storagetreasures.com/) | Largest storage auction platform | Online bidding; listings nationwide |
| [Lockerfox](https://www.lockerfox.com/) | Online storage unit auctions | App + website; most states covered |
| [StorageAuctions.com](https://www.storageauctions.com/) | Online storage lien sales | Nationwide listings |
| [Bid13](https://www.bid13.com/) | Storage unit auction platform | Canadian + US listings |

**Tips**: Units are sold as-is, contents unseen (photos of the open door
only). Bring your own lock for after winning. You must remove all contents
within 24–48 hours. Best for resellers — furniture, electronics, tools, and
collectibles can be flipped for profit. Look for units with visible quality
furniture, labeled boxes, or climate-controlled facility indicators.
