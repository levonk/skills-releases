# Situational Fit — Attribute Reference

Constraint attribute: applies when the item could mismatch the user's
environment, or when financing is involved. Referenced by
`references/constraint-attributes.md`.

## Climate Mismatch

| Mismatch | Example | Problem |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Generator for apartment | Can't run indoors; no outdoor space | Useless purchase |
| AC in cool climate | Rarely needed | Wasted money |
| Snow blower where it never snows | No snow | Useless purchase |
| Evaporative cooler in humid climate | Doesn't work above 50% humidity | Won't cool |
| Heat pump in extreme cold | Below -10°F, efficiency drops | Needs backup heat source |
| Solar panels in shade | No sun = no power | Won't generate |
| Wood stove in urban area | Smoke ordinances, no chimney | Can't install |

Check the user's climate zone and typical conditions before recommending
climate-dependent products.

## Infrastructure Dependency

| Product | Infrastructure needed | If missing |
|---------|----------------------|------------|
| EV | Home charging (240V outlet or charger) | Public charging is 3–5x more expensive and inconvenient |
| Fiber device | Fiber internet connection | Won't work; check availability at user's address |
| Well pump | Reliable grid power | Needs generator backup or battery |
| Smart home devices | Reliable internet | Devices become unreliable or non-functional |
| Heat pump dryer | 240V circuit | May need electrical upgrade ($500–$2,000) |
| Tankless water heater | Gas line + sufficient flow rate | May need gas line upgrade |
| Induction cooktop | 240V circuit + compatible cookware | Electrical + cookware upgrade costs |

Verify the user has the required infrastructure before recommending. If
infrastructure upgrades are needed, factor the upgrade cost into the total
purchase cost.

## Space & Installation Constraints

- **Will it fit through the door?** Measure doorways, hallways, stairs, and
  elevators. Common problem items: refrigerators, sofas, mattresses, washing
  machines.
- **Does it need ventilation?** Gas appliances, generators, some
  electronics require specific clearances.
- **Does it need a floor drain?** Washing machines, water heaters, AC units.
- **Does it need a dedicated circuit?** Microwaves, AC units, EV chargers,
  space heaters.
- **Does it need plumbing?** Ice makers, washing machines, dishwashers.
- **Weight limits?** Upper-floor appliances, wall-mounted TVs, rooftop AC.

Check installation requirements before recommending. An item that requires
$2,000 in electrical/plumbing upgrades isn't a deal at its sticker price.

## Financial Traps

### Store Credit Cards with Deferred Interest

"0% for 24 months" often means: if you don't pay in full by month 24, you
owe **all** back interest at 25%+ APR, calculated from the original purchase
date. Read the fine print:

- Is interest waived or deferred?
- What happens if you miss one payment?
- What's the regular APR after the promo period?

### Rent-to-Own Schemes

Total cost often 2–4x the retail price. Never recommend rent-to-own for
anything. If the user can't afford the purchase price, recommend saving or
buying used.

### Extended Warranty Upsells

Often priced at 20–40% of the product cost with claim denial rates above
50%. Usually not worth it unless:

- The product has known high failure rates
- The warranty is from the manufacturer (not a third party)
- The coverage is clearly defined and the claim process is simple

See `deal-intelligence/references/warranty-comparison.md` for warranty
comparison methodology.

### Financing Through the Seller

Furniture stores, auto dealers, and appliance retailers often offer
financing that appears cheap but has hidden costs:

- Prepayment penalties
- Balloon payments
- Required add-ons (gap insurance, extended warranty, paint protection)
- Higher negotiated price when financing vs cash

Always compare the financed total (all payments + fees) to the cash price.
