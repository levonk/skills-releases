# Water Heater Buying Constraints

> Tank, tankless, heat-pump. What's unique vs generic appliances: sizing is
> driven by **first-hour rating** (tank) or **GPM at temperature rise**
> (tankless), and venting/fuel type heavily constrains options.

## Tank vs Tankless

| Factor | Tank | Tankless |
|--------|------|----------|
| Upfront | $500-1500 | $1000-3500 |
| Lifespan | 10-15 yr | 20+ yr |
| Hot water | Limited by tank size | Unlimited (within GPM) |
| Standby loss | Yes (reheats all day) | None |
| Space | Larger floor footprint | Wall-mounted, compact |
| Maintenance | Anode rod check | Annual descale (hard water) |

## Fuel Type

| Fuel | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Gas (tank) | Cheapest to run; needs venting + gas line |
| Gas (tankless) | High GPM; needs large gas supply + venting |
| Electric (tank) | Simple install; higher operating cost in most markets |
| Electric (tankless) | Limited GPM; may need panel upgrade (120-160A) |
| Heat pump (hybrid) | $1500-3500, 2-3x efficiency; needs warm space (40°F+), makes noise, taller |

- Heat-pump water heaters **dehumidify and cool** the room — bad for
  conditioned spaces in winter, good in garages.

## Sizing — Tank

| Household | Gallons |
|-----------|---------|
| 1-2 people | 30-40 gal |
| 3-4 people | 40-50 gal |
| 5+ people | 50-80 gal |

- Use **first-hour rating** (FHR) — gallons of hot water delivered in the first
  hour — not just tank size. Match FHR to peak hour demand.

## Sizing — Tankless

- Sized by **GPM at the required temperature rise** (groundwater temp to set
  temp, typically 77°F rise).
- Add up simultaneous fixtures: shower ~2.0 GPM, faucet ~1.0, washer ~2.0.
- Example: 2 showers + 1 sink = 5 GPM at 77°F rise — needs a high-capacity unit.

## Venting

| Type | Applies to |
|------|-----------|
| Atmospheric | Gas tank, standard; vertical flue; least efficient |
| Power vent | Gas tank, horizontal sidewall vent; fan-assisted |
| Power Direct Vent | Gas tank/tankless, sealed combustion, two-pipe |
| Concentric | Tankless, single-pipe (inner exhaust / outer intake) |

- Confirm venting path before selecting a gas model — re-routing flues is
  expensive.

## Recirculation Pump

- Provides **instant hot water** at the tap — $200-600 installed.
- Reduces water waste; adds small standby energy cost.
- On-demand button or motion-sensor versions minimize energy penalty.

## Anode Rod (Tank)

- The anode rod is the sacrificial part that prevents tank corrosion.
- **Check annually**, replace when ~50% consumed — extends tank life by years.
- Powered anode rods ($100-200) last longer and work in softened water.

## Output Format Example

```
Type: 50-gal gas tank, power direct vent
Fuel: Natural gas
Sizing: 3-4 people, FHR 87 gal — covers peak shower+washer
Venting: Power direct vent (sealed combustion, sidewall)
First-hour rating: 87 gal
Recirculation: optional on-demand pump ($200-600)
Anode rod: check annually, replace at 50% wear
Lifespan: 10-15 yr
```
