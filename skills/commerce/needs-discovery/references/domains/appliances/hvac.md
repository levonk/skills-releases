# HVAC Buying Constraints

> Central AC, furnace, heat pump, mini-split. What's unique vs generic
> appliances: the **installation is the majority of the cost** and sizing is
> engineering-driven, not rule-of-thumb.

## Efficiency Ratings

| Rating | Applies to | Guidance |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| SEER2 | Central AC, heat pump (cooling) | Minimum 14 SEER2 nationally; 15+ recommended; 16+ for hot climates |
| HSPF2 | Heat pump (heating) | Minimum 8.8; 9.5+ recommended |
| AFUE | Furnace | 80% minimum; 90+ high-efficiency (condensing); 95+ best |
| EER2 | Room AC / mini-split | Room-unit efficiency; higher = better |

- Higher ratings cost more upfront but pay back over 10-15 yr in lower bills.

## Sizing — Manual J Calculation

- **Proper sizing requires an ACCA Manual J load calculation** (climate,
  insulation, windows, orientation, ceiling height).
- Oversized unit → short-cycles, poor dehumidification, comfort swings, wear.
- Undersized → can't hold setpoint on extreme days.
- Rule of thumb **1 ton per 400-600 sq ft** is a starting point only — never
  the basis for purchase.
- Heat pump sizing must cover **both** heating and cooling loads; in cold
  climates heating load governs.

## Refrigerant

| Refrigerant | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| R-410A | Being phased down (AIM Act) — still serviceable but production capped |
| R-32 | Coming — lower GWP, mildly flammable (A2L) |
| R-454B | Coming — Carrier/Trane transition refrigerant (A2L) |

- Ask the user if they want to future-proof against the R-410A phase-down.
- A2L refrigerants may require leak detection sensors in some jurisdictions.

## Ductwork

- Leaky ducts waste **20-30%** of conditioned air.
- Duct sealing runs **$500-2000**; often a better ROI than a higher-SEER unit.
- Have ducts inspected before replacing equipment — a new unit on bad ducts
  underperforms.

## Installation Cost

- Installation is often **50-70% of total system cost**.
- **Get 3+ quotes** from licensed contractors; price and quality vary widely.
- Verify the contractor does a Manual J, not just a tonnage swap of the old unit.

## Heat Pump — Cold Climate

- Below **-10°F**, most heat pumps lose efficiency and need **backup heat**
  (electric resistance strips or dual-fuel with a furnace).
- Cold-climate-rated heat pumps (NEEP-listed) maintain capacity to ~-13°F or
  lower.
- Ask the user's winter design temperature before recommending heat pump only.

## Mini-Split vs Central

| Factor | Mini-split | Central |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Ducts | None needed | Required (or existing) |
| Zoning | Native (per indoor unit) | Needs zoning dampers |
| Cost | $1.5k-5k per zone | $5k-15k total |
| Aesthetics | Wall cassettes visible | Vents only |
| Best for | Additions, no-duct homes, room-by-room control | Whole-home, existing ducts |

## Thermostat Compatibility

- Heat pump requires a **heat pump-compatible thermostat** (O/B terminal,
  emergency heat).
- Smart thermostats (ecobee, Nest) support most heat pumps — confirm wiring.

## Output Format Example

```
System: 3-ton central heat pump, 16 SEER2 / 9.5 HSPF2
Sizing: Manual J required — 3 ton est. for ~1500 sq ft, hot climate
Refrigerant: R-454B (future-proof)
Ductwork: inspect + seal before install ($500-2000 if needed)
Install: 50-70% of cost — get 3+ quotes
Cold climate: backup heat required below -10°F
Thermostat: heat-pump-compatible smart thermostat
```
