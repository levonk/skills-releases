# Refrigeration Buying Constraints

> Fridge, freezer. What's unique vs generic appliances: **configuration**
drives capacity and price, **sealed-system warranty** is the one that matters,
and **compressor type** affects noise and longevity.

## Fridge Configurations

| Config | Pros | Price range |
|--------|------|-------------|
| French door | Popular, wide shelves, eye-level fridge | $1500-4000 |
| Side-by-side | Narrow doors (tight spaces), more freezer | $1200-3000 |
| Top freezer | Cheapest, simple | $600-1200 |
| Bottom freezer | Convenient fridge access | $800-1800 |
| Counter-depth | Flush with cabinets | $300-800 premium, 20-30% less capacity |
| Built-in | Custom panels, true flush | $4000-10000+ |

- Counter-depth sacrifices **20-30% capacity** for the look — confirm the user
  prioritizes aesthetics over volume.

## Ice / Water Dispenser

- Adds **$200-500** to the purchase price.
- Requires **plumbing** (braided line + shutoff) within 6-8 ft.
- **Filter replacement**: $30-60 every 6 months — recurring cost.
- Through-door dispensers add a failure point and reduce door insulation
  slightly.

## Compressor Type

| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Standard (single-speed) | Cycles on/off; more temp swing, louder |
| Inverter (variable-speed) | Quieter, more efficient, longer-lasting, more stable temp |
| Dual compressor | Fridge + freezer on separate systems — better humidity control, no odor transfer; $500+ premium |

- **Inverter** is worth the premium for open-plan kitchens (noise) and
  longevity.
- **Dual compressor** matters most if the user stores odor-sensitive foods or
  wants the crispest produce humidity control.

## Warranty — Sealed System Is Key

| Coverage | Typical term |
|----------|-------------|
| General (parts/labor) | 1 yr |
| Sealed system (compressor, refrigerant lines, evaporator) | **5-7 yr** — this is the one that matters |
| Compressor (some brands) | 10 yr (parts only, labor extra after yr 1) |

- A sealed-system failure is a **$500-1000+ repair** — the warranty term here
  is more important than the 1-year general coverage.
- Confirm whether labor is covered for the full sealed-system term or only year
  1.

## Freezer — Chest vs Upright

| Factor | Chest | Upright |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Efficiency | More efficient (cold air settles) | Less efficient (escapes on door open) |
| Power outage hold | 2-3 days (full, unopened) | ~1 day |
| Organization | Harder (stacking, digging) | Easy (shelves, baskets) |
| Footprint | Wider, shorter | Taller, narrower |
| Frost-free | Rare (mostly manual) | Common option |

## Frost-Free vs Manual Defrost

| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Frost-free | Convenient; cycles to melt frost — causes **freezer burn faster** |
| Manual defrost | Better food preservation, lower energy; **defrost annually** |

- Long-term storage (months) → manual defrost. Convenience-first → frost-free.

## Garage-Ready

- Standard freezers/fridges struggle **below 38°F or above 110°F** (compressor
  won't run correctly).
- Garage-ready units are insulated and have a **heater on the thermostat** —
  $50-100 premium.
- Confirm the user's garage temperature range before placing a standard unit
  there.

## Output Format Example

```
Fridge: French door, counter-depth, 22 cu ft, inverter compressor
Dispenser: through-door ice/water, filter $40/6mo, plumbing required
Warranty: 1 yr general / 5 yr sealed system (labor yr 1 only)
Freezer: 14 cu ft upright, frost-free, garage-ready
Note: counter-depth = 20-30% less capacity than standard depth
```
