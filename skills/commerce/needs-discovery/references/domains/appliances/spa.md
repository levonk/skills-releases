# Spa Buying Constraints

> Sauna, jacuzzi / hot tub. What's unique vs generic appliances: **dedicated
> electrical** is almost always required, **water chemistry** is an ongoing
> cost and safety issue, and **permits/zoning** can block installation.

## Sauna

### Traditional vs Infrared

| Factor | Traditional | Infrared |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Temp | 170-190°F | 120-140°F |
| Heat source | Electric heater + rocks (steam possible) | Infrared panels |
| Experience | Hot, humid, steam | Lower temp, dry radiant heat |
| Cost | $3000-8000 | $1500-5000 |
| Electrical | 240V / 30-60A dedicated | 120V / 15-20A or 240V |
| Heat-up time | 30-45 min | 10-20 min |

### Wood Type

- **Cedar** — traditional, aromatic, rot-resistant, premium.
- **Hemlock** — common, affordable, less aromatic.
- **Aspen** — light, minimal odor, good for scent-sensitive users.
- **Avoid treated lumber** — chemical off-gassing at sauna temps is hazardous.

### Sauna Build Requirements

| Requirement | Guidance |
|-------------|----------|
| Ventilation | Fresh air intake low + exhaust high; ~6 sq in each |
| Electrical | Traditional: 240V/30-60A dedicated circuit; infrared: 120V/15-20A or 240V |
| Floor | Waterproof surface; **drain recommended** |
| Clearance | Per manufacturer — typically 2" from walls/ceiling for airflow |
| Maintenance | Wipe down after use; wood treatment annually; inspect heater |

## Jacuzzi / Hot Tub

### Electrical

- **240V / 50A dedicated circuit** is standard — install runs **$500-2000**.
- 120V "plug-and-play" tubs exist but heat slowly and limit jet count.
- GFCI disconnect required within sight of the tub.

### Water Chemistry (Ongoing)

| Parameter | Target | Test frequency |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| pH | 7.2-7.8 | Weekly |
| Sanitizer (chlorine/bromine) | Per product chart | Weekly |
| Total alkalinity | 80-120 ppm | Weekly |
| Calcium hardness | 150-250 ppm | Monthly |

- Chemical cost: **$20-40/month**.
- Neglected chemistry → skin irritation, equipment damage, cloudy water.

### Cover & Filters

| Item | Cost | Replacement |
|------|------|-------------|
| Insulated cover | $200-600 | Every 5-7 yr |
| Filter cartridge(s) | $30-100 | Every 6-12 months |

- A good cover is the **single biggest energy saver** — replace if waterlogged.

### Water Change

- Drain and refill every **3-4 months** (typical tub: 300-500 gal).
- More often with heavy use or persistent chemistry issues.

### Installation & Siting

| Requirement | Guidance |
|-------------|----------|
| Base | Level pad — concrete slab or reinforced deck (500-1000 lb filled) |
| Delivery access | Side gate width, fence removal, crane if rooftop/upper deck |
| Drainage | Plan where 300-500 gal drains (storm sewer OK, sanitary preferred) |
| Clearance | 2-3 ft on all sides for cover lift + service |

### Zoning / Permits

- Many areas require a **fence or barrier** (often 4-5 ft) around the tub or
  yard.
- Electrical permit + inspection typically required.
- Some HOAs restrict hot tubs — check before purchase.

### Energy Cost

- **$20-60/month** depending on climate, cover quality, and usage.
- Cold climates and poor covers push toward the high end.

## Output Format Example

```
Sauna: outdoor traditional, 240V/40A dedicated, cedar, 6-person
Ventilation: low intake + high exhaust, drain in floor
Hot tub: 240V/50A, 350 gal, 6-person, insulated cover ($400, 5-7 yr life)
Chemistry: weekly test, $30/mo chemicals, drain/refill every 3-4 mo
Permits: electrical permit + 5 ft yard fence required locally
Energy: est. $40/mo (mild climate, good cover)
```
