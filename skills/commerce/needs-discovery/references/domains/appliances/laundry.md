# Laundry Buying Constraints

> Washer, dryer, combo. What's unique vs generic appliances: **venting**
> (dryer), **HE detergent** requirement, spin-speed's effect on drying, and
> mold risk on front-loaders.

## Washer — Front-Load vs Top-Load

| Factor | Front-load | Top-load (impeller) | Top-load (agitator) |
|-------|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Cleaning | Better | Good | Fair-good |
| Water use | Low | Medium | High |
| Cycle time | Longer (60-90 min) | Medium (45-60 min) | Shortest (30-45 min) |
| Mold risk | High if door closed | Low | Low |
| Cost | Higher | Medium | Lowest |
| Ergonomics | Low door (bending) | Easy top access | Easy top access |

- Front-load: **leave door ajar** between loads; clean gasket monthly to avoid
  mildew.
- Agitator models are fading but cheapest; impeller (low-profile) is the modern
  top-load design.

## Washer — Capacity

| Capacity | Fits |
|----------|------|
| < 4.0 cu ft | Small loads, apartments |
| 4.0-4.4 cu ft | Typical family |
| 4.5+ cu ft | King-size bedding, comforters |
| 5.0+ cu ft | Large families, frequent bulk loads |

## Washer — HE Detergent

- High-efficiency washers **require HE detergent** — over-sudsing with regular
  detergent damages the machine and voids warranty.
- Use the **minimum** recommended amount; most users over-dose.

## Washer — Spin Speed

- Higher RPM extracts more water → **less dryer time and energy**.
- Typical: 800-1400 RPM. 1200+ RPM is good; 1400+ is high-performance.
- Trade-off: very high spin increases wrinkling on delicate fabrics.

## Dryer — Gas vs Electric

| Factor | Gas | Electric |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Operating cost | Lower (where gas is cheap) | Higher |
| Install | Gas line + venting | 240V outlet + venting |
| Heat response | Fast | Slower |
| Best when | Gas line already present | No gas available |

- **Heat pump dryer**: ventless, ~50% less energy, gentler on clothes, but
  slower cycles and $300-800 more upfront. Good for apartments / no-vent
  installs.

## Dryer — Venting

- Use **rigid metal duct** (not flexible foil/plastic — fire risk).
- Max run **25 ft** (subtract 5 ft per 90° elbow).
- **Clean annually** — lint buildup is a leading fire cause.
- Heat-pump dryers are ventless — no duct needed.

## Dryer — Moisture Sensor

- Sensor stops the cycle when clothes are dry → saves energy and reduces
  overdrying damage.
- Prefer **dual-sensor** (drum + outlet) over timer-only models.

## Combo Washer-Dryer

- **One machine** washes then dries — ventless condensing.
- Best for **small spaces without venting** (apartments, ADUs, RVs).
- Trade-offs: smaller capacity (2.0-4.5 cu ft), longer cycles (2-3 hr),
  reliability concerns, can't wash a second load while drying.
- Not recommended as a primary laundry for a family.

## Output Format Example

```
Washer: 4.8 cu ft front-load, 1300 RPM, HE detergent required
Dryer: 7.4 cu ft gas, moisture-sensor, rigid metal vent (max 25 ft, clean annually)
Pair note: front-load washer — leave door ajar, clean gasket monthly
Combo alt: ventless 2.4 cu ft combo — only if no venting possible
```
