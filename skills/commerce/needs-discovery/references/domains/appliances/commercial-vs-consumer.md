# Commercial vs Consumer Appliances

> When to buy commercial-grade vs consumer-grade. What's unique vs generic
> appliance guidance: **duty cycle**, **NSF certification**, **electrical
> service**, and **warranty voiding** when the wrong class is used.

## Key Differences

| Factor | Commercial | Consumer |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Durability | 10-15+ yr daily use | 5-10 yr |
| Warranty | 1-3 yr parts, **on-site service** | 1 yr, carry-in |
| Electrical | 208V/240V single or **three-phase**; may need upgrade | 120V or 240V single phase |
| NSF certification | Required for food service | Not required |
| Noise | Louder — no sound insulation | 44-50 dB (quiet) |
| Cost | **2-4x** consumer price | Baseline |
| Parts availability | Same-day from restaurant supply | 3-10 days |
| Repair access | Any certified tech | Manufacturer-authorized only (for warranty) |
| Intended use | Continuous daily operation | Intermittent family use |

## NSF Certification

- **NSF International** certifies equipment for food service.
- Requirements: **stainless steel** construction, washable surfaces, **no
  exposed threads** or crevices, coved corners, food-grade materials.
- Required by most **health departments** for any commercial food operation.
- A consumer fridge in a restaurant will **fail inspection**.

## Electrical Service

- Commercial equipment often needs **208V or 240V**, and larger units may
  require **three-phase** power.
- Residential service is **single-phase 120/240V** — a three-phase unit won't
  run without an upgrade or a phase converter.
- Budget **$1500-5000+** for an electrical service upgrade if needed.

## Warranty — The Duty-Cycle Trap

- Consumer appliances used in a **commercial setting** **void the warranty** —
  manufacturers specify household use.
- A consumer range in a church kitchen or Airbnb with heavy turnover may not be
  covered if the brand investigates the claim.
- Commercial warranties include **on-site service** — consumer warranties are
  typically carry-in or limited on-site for the first year only.

## Noise

- Commercial units are built for **back-of-house** — no sound insulation,
  louder compressors, metal-to-metal contact.
- In a home kitchen or open-plan space, a commercial dishwasher or fridge may
  be **unacceptably loud**.

## When to Buy Commercial

- **Restaurant / cafe / food truck** — NSF required, duty cycle demands it.
- **Large family that cooks daily** — pro-style range (often "prosumer" rather
  than true commercial) may be worth it.
- **Off-grid / durability priority** — commercial units tolerate abuse and
  have parts availability.
- **Shared / rental kitchen** — warranty must cover heavy use.

## When Consumer Is Fine

- **Normal household use** — consumer appliances are designed for it and
  cost 2-4x less.
- **Quiet matters** — consumer dishwashers and fridges are far quieter.
- **No three-phase / 208V available** — avoid the electrical upgrade cost.
- **Budget-constrained** — the durability premium rarely pays back for a family
  of 4 cooking 1-2 meals/day.

## Prosumer / Pro-Style (Middle Ground)

- Brands like Wolf, Thermador, JennAir offer **pro-style** ranges with
  consumer-grade warranty and electrical (240V single-phase), but commercial
  aesthetics and heavier build.
- Good fit for serious home cooks who want durability without commercial
  electrical/noise trade-offs.

## Output Format Example

```
Decision: consumer (normal household, quiet matters, no 208V)
Rationale: 2-4x cheaper, warranty valid for household use, 44 dB dishwasher
If commercial needed: NSF-certified, 208V/3-phase, on-site warranty, $2-4x cost
Prosumer alt: pro-style range (240V 1-phase, consumer warranty) for serious home cook
```
