# Commercial Lease — Sub-Domain Reference

Constraints specific to leasing commercial space (retail, office, industrial).
Referenced by `leasee/index.md`. Load in addition to the generic tenant
constraints.

Commercial leasing is fundamentally different from residential — the lease
is a business contract, the terms are negotiable, and the financial
commitment is much larger.

## Lease Types

| Lease type | What tenant pays | Budget for |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **Triple net (NNN)** | Base + taxes + insurance + maintenance | Base + ~$3–$15/sq ft/yr in NNN charges |
| **Double net (NN)** | Base + taxes + insurance | Maintenance + structure are landlord's |
| **Gross / full-service** | Base rent only | Utilities, janitorial, internet separately |
| **Modified gross** | Base + some expenses | Read the lease carefully — varies |
| **Industrial gross** | Base + utilities + janitorial | Taxes, insurance, structure are landlord's |

**NNN risk**: NNN charges can increase unpredictably (tax reassessment,
insurance premium increases, major roof replacement). Ask for historical
NNN charges and cap projections. Negotiate a cap on NNN increases (e.g.,
max 5%/year).

## Lease Term

| Term type | Typical duration | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| **Short-term** | 1–3 years | Higher rent, more flexibility |
| **Standard** | 3–5 years | Common for small businesses |
| **Long-term** | 5–10+ years | Lower rent, but tenant commits to location |

## Key Commercial Lease Provisions

| Provision | What to check | Red flag |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Renewal option** | Terms, notice required, rent at renewal | No renewal option, or renewal at "market" with no cap |
| **Exclusive use** | Tenant's exclusive right to operate a type of business | No exclusive use in a shopping center |
| **Co-tenancy** | Rent reduction if anchor tenant leaves | No co-tenancy clause |
| **TI allowance** | $/sq ft for build-out | No TI allowance for multi-year lease |
| **Assignment/sublease** | Right to assign or sublease | No assignment without landlord consent (unreasonable) |
| **Holdover rent** | Rent if tenant stays past lease end | > 150% of base rent (penalty holdover) |
| **Personal guarantee** | Is personal guarantee required? | Unlimited personal guarantee for a corporation |
| **Restoration** | Tenant must restore to original condition at end | Must remove all TI improvements |
| **Force majeure** | What events excuse performance | No force majeure clause (post-COVID, this matters) |
| **Relocation clause** | Landlord can move tenant to another unit | Open-ended relocation (no cost coverage, no size guarantee) |
| **Right of first refusal** | Tenant can match any purchase offer | No ROFR — landlord can sell to anyone |

## Hidden Costs (Commercial)

- **NNN charges**: $3–$15/sq ft/yr on top of base rent
- **Utilities**: Electric, gas, water, internet (may be separate metered)
- **Janitorial**: If not included in lease, $1–$2/sq ft/yr
- **Parking**: $50–$200/space/month in urban areas
- **Common area maintenance (CAM)**: May include snow removal, landscaping,
  security, elevator maintenance
- **Percentage rent**: If retail, % of gross sales above a breakpoint
- **Insurance**: Tenant must carry liability insurance ($500–$2,000/yr)
- **Property tax pass-through**: In NNN leases, tenant pays proportionate
  share of property tax increases

## Tenant Improvement (TI) Negotiation

Commercial tenants often require build-out of the space. Negotiate who pays:

| TI scenario | Who pays | Impact on tenant |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Turnkey** | Landlord pays all | Higher rent, but no upfront cost |
| **Tenant allowance ($/sq ft)** | Landlord pays up to $X/sq ft | Capped cost; tenant pays overage |
| **Tenant pays all** | Tenant | Lower rent, but large upfront cost |

TI costs: $20–$100+/sq ft depending on build-out quality. For a 2,000 sq ft
office at $50/sq ft, that's $100,000 in build-out costs. Negotiate TI
allowance into the lease — typically $10–$40/sq ft for office, $30–$80/sq ft
for retail.

## Negotiation Leverage (Commercial)

- **Tenant improvement allowance**: Negotiate $/sq ft for build-out
- **Free rent**: 1–3 months free rent at start of lease (common for
  multi-year commercial leases)
- **Rent abatement**: Reduced rent during the first year (graduated lease)
- **Renewal option at capped increase**: Lock in future rent increases
  (e.g., CPI capped at 3%)
- **Exclusivity clause**: In retail, prevent landlord from leasing to a
  direct competitor
- **Co-tenancy clause**: Rent reduction or termination right if anchor
  tenant leaves (critical in shopping centers)
- **Personal guarantee limitation**: Negotiate a burn-off (guarantee
  expires after 2–3 years of on-time rent) or cap the guarantee amount

## Parking (Commercial)

| Parking type | Typical cost | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Included in lease** | $0 | Check ratio: 1–4 spaces per 1,000 sq ft depending on use |
| **Additional spaces** | $50–$200/space/month | Urban areas; may have waitlist |
| **Validation for guests** | $2–$10/visit | Check if building offers validation |
| **EV charging** | Varies | Check if building has EV stations |
| **Customer parking** | Critical for retail | If no customer parking, foot traffic may suffer |

**For retail**: Customer parking is critical. A retail space without
dedicated customer parking in a non-walkable area will struggle. Check
parking ratio (spaces per 1,000 sq ft) and whether parking is shared with
other tenants.
