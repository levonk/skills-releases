# Apartment Rental — Sub-Domain Reference

Constraints specific to renting an apartment in a multi-unit building.
Referenced by `leasee/index.md`. Load in addition to the generic tenant
constraints.

Apartment renting differs from home renting in several key ways: shared
walls and noise, building amenities, parking scarcity, management company
quality, move-in logistics, and different maintenance expectations.

## Noise

The #1 complaint in apartment living. Check before signing:

| Noise source | How to check | Red flag |
|--------------|-------------|----------|
| **Upstairs neighbors** | Visit during evening hours; listen for footsteps | Hardwood floors above (carpet dampens; wood doesn't) |
| **Shared walls** | Knock on shared wall — how thick does it sound? | Thin walls; you can hear TV/conversations |
| **Hallway noise** | Is the unit near the elevator, stairwell, or entrance? | High-traffic hallway = constant door slamming |
| **Street noise** | Check window facing street; listen with window open and closed | Busy street, fire station, hospital, highway |
| **Garbage chute** | Is the unit near the chute? | Noise and smell from chute usage |
| **Laundry room** | Is the unit adjacent to shared laundry? | Vibration and noise during operation |
| **Gym/pool** | Is the unit above or adjacent to amenities? | Noise during operating hours |
| **HVAC** | Is the unit's AC compressor on the balcony or shared? | Compressor noise during summer |

**Pro tip**: Ask the current tenant (if available) about noise. Visit the
unit at 7 PM on a weekday and 10 PM on a weekend — these are peak noise
hours.

## Parking

Apartment parking is often scarce and expensive:

| Parking type | Typical cost | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Assigned spot (covered)** | $50–$200/month | Guaranteed; usually in a garage or carport |
| **Assigned spot (uncovered)** | $30–$100/month | Guaranteed; open lot |
| **Unassigned (first come)** | $0–$50/month | Not guaranteed; may need to park far away |
| **Guest parking** | $0–$20/visit | Often limited to 2–4 hours or requires permit |
| **Street parking** | Free or permit | May be far from building; check neighborhood permit rules |
| **No parking included** | Market rate | Urban areas — may need to rent a spot separately ($100–$400/month) |

**Key questions**:
- How many spots are included in the rent?
- Is guest parking available? How many spots? Time limit?
- Is there a waitlist for assigned spots?
- Is the parking garage secure (gated, cameras)?
- Can you charge an EV? Are there EV stations in the garage?

## Amenities

| Amenity | Value | Hidden cost |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| **In-unit laundry** | High — saves $20–$40/month + time | Check if included or extra |
| **Shared laundry** | Low — $2–$4 per load | Check number of machines vs units (1:10 ratio minimum) |
| **Gym** | Medium — saves $30–$60/month | Check hours, equipment quality, crowding |
| **Pool** | Medium — seasonal value | Check hours, cleanliness, crowding |
| **Package locker** | High — prevents theft | Check if Amazon/UPS/USPS all deliver to it |
| **Bike storage** | Medium — saves $50–$100/month indoor storage | Check security |
| **Storage unit** | Medium — $50–$150/month value | Check if included or extra |
| **Roof deck / courtyard** | Low-medium — quality of life | Check hours, rules |
| **Concierge / doorman** | Medium — package security, guest access | Usually in luxury buildings only |
| **EV charging** | High if you have an EV | Check if included or $/kWh rate |

**Amenities are baked into rent** — a building with a pool, gym, and
concierge charges $200–$500/month more than a comparable building without.
Don't pay for amenities you won't use.

## Building Management

| Management type | Pros | Cons |
|----------------|------|------|
| **On-site manager** | Fast response, knows building | May be intrusive; limited availability |
| **Property management company** | Professional, 24/7 maintenance line | Slow for non-urgent; high turnover in staff |
| **Corporate (institutional)** | Professional, well-funded, app-based requests | Least flexible; rent increases at renewal |
| **Small landlord (2–20 units)** | Personal, flexible | Slow for major repairs; inconsistent |

**Check reviews**: Search [ApartmentRatings](https://www.apartmentratings.com/),
Google Reviews, and Yelp for the building and management company. Look for
patterns, not individual complaints:
- Maintenance response time (should be < 48 hours for non-emergency)
- Rent increase history at renewal
- Security deposit return issues
- Pest problems
- Noise complaints
- Management turnover (high turnover = unstable)

## Move-In Logistics

Apartments have more complex move-in logistics than houses:

| Factor | What to check | Cost if ignored |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Elevator reservation** | Reserve freight elevator for move-in day | $50–$200 fee if not reserved; may be blocked |
| **Move-in fee** | Some buildings charge a non-refundable move-in fee | $200–$500 |
| **Move-in hours** | Buildings restrict move-in to certain hours/days | Fines for violating; typically 9 AM–5 PM weekdays |
| **Stairs** | Walk-up units (no elevator) — can you carry furniture? | Movers charge extra for walk-ups ($50–$200 per floor) |
| **Truck access** | Is there a loading dock? Street access? Height clearance? | May need to park far away and carry |
| **Insurance** | Buildings often require liability insurance before move-in | $15–$30/month; must provide certificate |
| **Pet move-in** | Pet deposit + pet rent + breed/weight restrictions | $200–$500 deposit + $25–$50/month |

## Unit-Specific Checks

| Check | What to look for | Why it matters |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Water pressure** | Run all faucets + shower simultaneously | Low pressure = daily frustration |
| **Drainage** | Flush toilet, run sink — does it drain fast? | Slow drains = future clogs |
| **Heat/AC** | Test both — does it reach all rooms? | Some units have poor airflow to bedrooms |
| **Outlets** | Count them; check placement | Older buildings have too few outlets |
| **GFI outlets** | Check kitchen and bathroom | Missing GFI = safety hazard |
| **Windows** | Open/close all; check seals and locks | Drafty windows = high utility bills; broken locks = security |
| **Closet space** | Measure — apartments often have less than houses | May need to buy storage furniture |
| **Lighting** | Check what's included vs what you need to provide | Some apartments have no ceiling lights |
| **Cell signal** | Check signal in all rooms | Concrete/steel buildings can block signal |
| **Internet** | What providers serve the building? | Some buildings have exclusive deals (monopoly pricing) |
| **Pest signs** | Check kitchen cabinets, baseboards, under sink | Droppings, traps, gel bait = existing problem |

## Lease Renewal and Rent Increases

| Factor | What to expect | Negotiation tip |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Renewal offer timing** | 60–90 days before lease end | Respond early — waiting may trigger auto-non-renewal |
| **Typical increase** | 3–10% depending on market | Research comparable rents in the area |
| **Concession leverage** | If building has vacancies, you have leverage | Ask for no increase or free month |
| **New tenant vs renewal** | Some buildings offer lower rent to new tenants than renewals | Threaten to leave — they may match |
| **Month-to-month premium** | Usually 10–25% more than lease rate | If you need flexibility, negotiate the premium down |
