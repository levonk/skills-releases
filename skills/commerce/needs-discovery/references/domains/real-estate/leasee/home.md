# Home Rental — Sub-Domain Reference

Constraints specific to renting a house, townhouse, or condo. Referenced by
`leasee/index.md`. Load in addition to the generic tenant constraints.

Home renting differs from apartment renting in several key ways: the tenant
is usually responsible for more maintenance, utilities are higher, parking
is typically on-site, and the landlord is often a private individual rather
than a property management company.

## Maintenance Responsibility

Unlike apartments where the landlord handles all maintenance, home rentals
often split responsibilities:

| Responsibility | Typically landlord | Typically tenant | Check the lease |
|---------------|--------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Roof, foundation, structural** | ✅ | ❌ | Always landlord |
| **HVAC repair/replacement** | ✅ | ❌ | Always landlord |
| **Plumbing (main lines)** | ✅ | ❌ | Always landlord |
| **Appliance repair** | ✅ | ❌ | If provided by landlord |
| **Lawn mowing / yard work** | ❌ | ✅ | Usually tenant — verify |
| **Snow removal** | ❌ | ✅ | Usually tenant — verify |
| **Pest control** | Varies | Varies | Check lease — often tenant in houses |
| **Light bulbs, filters, batteries** | ❌ | ✅ | Tenant consumables |
| **Gutter cleaning** | ❌ | ✅ | Usually tenant — verify |
| **Tree trimming** | Varies | Varies | Large trees = landlord; small = tenant |
| **Fence repair** | Varies | Varies | Check lease |
| **Pool maintenance** | Varies | Varies | If pool exists — often tenant |

**Key question**: Who is responsible for yard maintenance? In most home
rentals, the tenant handles mowing, watering, and basic landscaping. If the
tenant doesn't want yard work, negotiate landscaping service into the lease
($100–$300/month depending on lot size).

## Utilities

Home rentals typically have higher utility costs than apartments (more
square footage, single-pane windows in older homes, less insulation):

| Utility | Home rental (monthly) | Notes |
|---------|----------------------|-------|
| **Electricity** | $100–$400 | Larger space, AC, appliances |
| **Gas** | $30–$200 | Heating, stove, water heater |
| **Water** | $40–$150 | Lawn irrigation adds significantly |
| **Trash** | $20–$50 | Often separate service |
| **Internet** | $50–$100 | Standard |
| **Total** | $240–$900 | vs $100–$300 for apartments |

**Ask for 12 months of utility bills** from the landlord or utility company
to estimate actual costs. A 2,000 sq ft house with poor insulation can cost
$400+/month in winter heating.

## Parking

Home rentals typically include on-site parking, which is a major advantage
over apartments:

| Parking type | Typical for home rental | Notes |
|--------------|------------------------|-------|
| **Driveway** | ✅ Included | 1–4 spaces; check if shared with other units |
| **Garage** | Sometimes | May be included or $50–$150/month extra; check if landlord uses it for storage |
| **Street parking** | Usually available | Check neighborhood permit requirements |
| **Guest parking** | Street or driveway | No assigned guest spots — guests park on street |

**Neighborhood parking restrictions**: Some neighborhoods have permit-only
parking, 2-hour limits, or street sweeping schedules that restrict when
vehicles can be parked. Check city parking regulations for the address.

## Landlord Type

| Landlord type | Pros | Cons |
|--------------|------|------|
| **Private individual** | Personal relationship, flexible negotiations, faster response for small issues | Slower for major repairs, may lack emergency fund, inconsistent communication |
| **Property management company** | Professional processes, 24/7 maintenance line, standardized lease | Less flexible, slower for non-urgent requests, fees for everything |
| **Small portfolio (2–10 units)** | Middle ground — semi-professional but still personal | Varies widely |
| **Corporate (institutional investor)** | Professional, well-funded, fast repairs | Least flexible, corporate lease, rent increases at renewal |

**For private landlords**: Check their responsiveness before signing. Ask
current/past tenants about repair turnaround time. A private landlord
without a maintenance fund may delay major repairs for months.

## Privacy

Home rentals offer significantly more privacy than apartments:

- No shared walls, ceilings, or floors
- Private yard
- No common areas
- No neighbor noise through walls
- Landlord must give notice before entering (typically 24–48 hours, varies
  by state)

**Trade-off**: More space and privacy, but more maintenance responsibility
and higher utility costs.

## HOA Considerations (If Townhouse/Condo)

If renting a townhouse or condo in an HOA community:

- **HOA rules apply to the tenant** — the landlord is responsible for
  ensuring compliance, but violations can result in fines passed through to
  the tenant
- **Guest parking restrictions** — HOAs may limit guest parking to specific
  areas or time limits
- **Amenity access** — Verify the tenant has access to HOA amenities (pool,
  gym, clubhouse) — some HOAs restrict tenant access
- **Pet restrictions** — HOA may have breed/size limits beyond what the
  landlord allows
- **Noise rules** — HOA may have stricter quiet hours than local ordinances

## Neighborhood Considerations

When renting a home, the neighborhood matters more than for apartments
because the tenant is there longer and has more outdoor exposure:

- **Walkability** — Can you walk to stores, schools, parks? Check
  [Walk Score](https://www.walkscore.com/)
- **School district** — Even if you don't have children, school district
  affects property values and the landlord's investment (which affects
  renewal stability)
- **Crime** — Check [CrimeReports](https://www.crimereports.com/) or local
  police department crime map
- **Noise** — Visit at different times: weekday morning, weekday evening,
  weekend night. Check for: barking dogs, traffic, construction, nearby
  bars/venues
- **Flood risk** — Check FEMA flood map — a rental house in a flood zone
  means potential evacuation and property damage to your belongings
- **Neighborhood parking** — Even with a driveway, check if street parking
  is available for guests and whether there are permit requirements
