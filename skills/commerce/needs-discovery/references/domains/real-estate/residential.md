# Residential Real Estate — Sub-Domain Reference

Constraints specific to buying a home to live in (primary or secondary
residence). Referenced by `domains/real-estate/index.md`. Load in addition
to the generic real estate constraints.

## Location Quality

- **School district**: Even if the user doesn't have children, school
  district quality is the single biggest driver of resale value and
  appreciation. Check [GreatSchools](https://www.greatschools.org/) ratings
  and district boundaries (which may not align with city boundaries). A
  home in a top-rated district holds value better and sells faster.
- **Commute**: Verify commute time to the user's workplace at the actual
  commute hours (not off-peak). Check transit availability. A 45-minute
  off-peak drive can be 90 minutes at rush hour. Use Google Maps commute
  planner with departure time set to 7:30 AM and 5:00 PM.
- **Neighborhood character**: Visit at different times (weekday morning,
  weekday evening, weekend night). Noise, parking, foot traffic, and
  atmosphere change dramatically by time of day. Check for nearby
  nuisances: bars (noise), fire stations (sirens), train tracks, airports,
  highways, industrial facilities, construction sites.
- **Walkability**: Check [Walk Score](https://www.walkscore.com/). Higher
  walkability correlates with resale value and quality of life.
- **Future development**: Check the city planning department for planned
  developments nearby — a new shopping center can increase value; a new
  highway or high-density housing can decrease it. Check for planned road
  expansions, rezoning, or eminent domain projects.

## Parking

Parking affects daily quality of life, resale value, and guest convenience.
Check both on-property and neighborhood parking:

| Parking type | What to check | Impact |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| **Driveway** | Length (fits how many cars?), shared with neighbor?, condition (cracks, drainage) | Primary parking for household |
| **Garage** | Attached/detached, 1-car/2-car/3-car, door opener age, interior access to house | Adds $5,000–$30,000 to value; check if used for storage (no car space) |
| **Carport** | Covered?, how many spaces? | Better than nothing; less secure than garage |
| **Street parking** | Permit required?, time limits?, street sweeping schedule?, snow emergency route? | Guest parking; may be restricted |
| **Neighborhood parking** | Is street parking readily available for guests? Or is it all permit/2-hour limit? | Affects entertainability; guests may get ticketed |
| **RV/boat/trailer** | HOA or city restrictions on parking RVs, boats, trailers in driveway/street? | Some HOAs prohibit; some cities limit to 24–72 hours |
| **EV charging** | Garage/driveway has 240V outlet or charger? Or easy to install? | Adding a 240V circuit costs $500–$2,000 |
| **ADA accessibility** | Is there a step-free path from parking to entrance? | Matters for aging in place or disabled residents |

**HOA parking rules**: If the property is in an HOA, check the CC&Rs for:
- Guest parking restrictions (time limits, designated spots)
- Commercial vehicle restrictions (no trucks over a certain weight, no
  company logos visible)
- RV/boat/trailer parking rules (often prohibited in driveway; may have
  designated storage area)
- Number of vehicles per household limit
- Driveway parking only (no street parking in some HOAs)

**Neighborhood parking red flags**:
- Permit-only parking with long waitlist for permits
- 2-hour visitor limits (guests must move cars every 2 hours)
- No street parking during winter (snow routes)
- Street sweeping twice per week (can't park for 4+ hours, 2 days/week)
- Apartment buildings nearby with insufficient parking (overflow into
  residential streets)

## Property Condition

- **Inspection**: Always recommend a professional home inspection ($300–$600).
  Major systems to inspect: roof, foundation, electrical, plumbing, HVAC,
  water heater, appliances, windows, doors, insulation, ventilation.
- **Roof**: Age and remaining life. Asphalt shingle: 15–30 years. Metal:
  40–70 years. Tile: 50+ years. Replacement cost: $5,000–$15,000+.
- **Foundation**: Look for horizontal cracks (serious), vertical cracks
  (monitor), uneven floors, doors that don't close properly. Foundation
  repair: $2,000–$20,000+.
- **HVAC**: Age and efficiency. Furnace: 15–25 years. AC: 10–15 years. Heat
  pump: 10–15 years. Replacement: $3,000–$10,000+.
- **Electrical**: Panel age and capacity (100A vs 200A). Aluminum wiring
  (1965–1973) is a fire risk and may require remediation. Knob-and-tube
  wiring (pre-1950) is obsolete and may be uninsurable.
- **Plumbing**: Galvanized steel (pre-1960) corrodes from inside. Polybutylene
  (1978–1995) fails unpredictably. Cast iron sewer lines last 50–100 years.
- **Water heater**: Age (8–12 year lifespan), tank vs tankless.
- **Well water**: Test for bacteria, nitrates, arsenic, lead, uranium. Well
  pump age and flow rate.
- **Septic system**: Inspect and pump. Check leach field condition. Septic
  replacement: $5,000–$30,000.

## HOA Livability (If Applicable)

- **Rules**: Read the full CC&R — not just the summary. Look for
  restrictions on: pets (size/breed limits), fencing, exterior paint
  colors, satellite dishes, solar panels, RV/boat parking, home business,
  renting, short-term rentals (Airbnb).
- **Fees**: Monthly/annual HOA dues. What do they cover? How often have
  they increased? Is there a special assessment pending?
- **Reserve study**: Does the HOA have adequate reserves for major repairs
  (roof, roads, pool)? An underfunded HOA means special assessments are
  coming — potentially $10,000–$50,000+ per unit.
- **Governance**: Review board meeting minutes for the past 12 months. Look
  for: ongoing disputes, litigation, deferred maintenance, dysfunctional
  board dynamics.
- **Rental restrictions**: If the user might rent the home later, check
  rental caps (many HOAs limit the percentage of units that can be rented).

## Financing

- **Mortgage rate**: Check current rates and whether the user should lock
  or float. Recommend comparing offers from at least 3 lenders (bank,
  credit union, mortgage broker).
- **PMI**: If down payment < 20%, private mortgage insurance adds
  $50–$300/month. Calculate when it can be removed.
- **Closing costs**: Typically 2–5% of purchase price. Budget for these
  in addition to the down payment.
- **Property tax escrow**: Verify the tax amount the lender will escrow —
  it may be based on the previous owner's assessed value, not the purchase
  price (especially in Prop 13 states).

## Resale Considerations

- **Days on market**: Check average DOM for the neighborhood. High DOM
  (> 60 days) suggests a buyer's market or undesirable area.
- **Price trends**: Check [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/),
  [Redfin](https://www.redfin.com/), or local MLS data for 1-year, 5-year,
  and 10-year price trends.
- **Comparable sales**: Review recent sales within 0.5 miles, similar
  size/condition, within the last 6 months. This is what an appraiser will
  use.
