# Yard Tools — Domain Reference

Domain-specific constraints for yard tools: mowers, trimmers, blowers,
chainsaws, hedge trimmers, tillers, and snow blowers. Referenced by
`references/constraint-attributes.md`. Load when the user is buying outdoor
power equipment. Power source, battery ecosystem, and yard size matching
drive the decision.

## Power Source

| Source | Power | Runtime | Noise | Maintenance | Emissions |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Gas | Highest | Unlimited (refill) | 85–105 dB | High (oil, filters, plugs) | Yes |
| Battery | High (improving) | 20–60 min | 65–80 dB | Low (battery care) | None |
| Corded electric | Medium | Unlimited (plugged in) | 60–75 dB | Minimal | None |

- **Gas**: Most power, unlimited runtime. Noisy (85–105 dB — hearing
  protection required). Best for large properties and heavy-duty tasks.
- **Battery**: Convenient, quiet, zero emissions. Modern 40V–80V mowers rival
  gas for residential use. Runtime 20–60 min; battery replacement $80–$200
  every 3–5 years. Best for small to medium yards.
- **Corded**: Unlimited runtime, lowest cost. Limited by cord. Small yards.

## Battery Ecosystem

**Buy into one brand platform — batteries work across tools.** Batteries
aren't interchangeable between brands; a typical setup needs 2–4 batteries
at $100–$200 each — often more than the bare tools.

| Platform | Voltage | Tool selection | Positioning |
|----------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| EGO | 56V | Excellent (mowers, blowers, chainsaws) | Premium residential |
| Greenworks | 60–80V | Good | Mid-range residential |
| DeWalt FlexVolt | 60V | Excellent (also 20V tools) | Pro/prosumer |
| Milwaukee M18 | 18V | Excellent (hand + outdoor) | Professional |
| Ryobi | 40V | Very wide selection | Budget residential |
| Stihl AP System | 36V | Good (pro-focused) | Professional |

## Cutting Width & Deck Size

| Tool | Range | Trade-off |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Mowers | 14–22 in | Wider = fewer passes but heavier, harder to maneuver |
| String trimmers | 12–18 in swath | Wider = faster but uses more line |
| Chainsaws | 12–20 in bar | Longer = bigger cuts but heavier, more kickback risk |
| Hedge trimmers | 18–30 in blade | Longer = faster on long hedges, less maneuverable |
| Snow blowers | 18–28 in | Wider = faster clearing but heavier |

## Runtime vs Charge Time

| Tool | Battery runtime | Charge time | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------------|-------|
| Mower | 30–60 min | 30–60 min | Second battery for >0.25 acre |
| String trimmer | 20–45 min | 30–60 min | |
| Blower | 15–40 min | 30–60 min | High-speed mode drains faster |
| Chainsaw | 20–40 min | 30–60 min | Hardwood cutting drains faster |
| Snow blower | 20–40 min | 30–60 min | Cold reduces capacity 10–20% |

**Second battery** eliminates downtime for large yards ($100–$200). Fast
chargers may reduce battery lifespan.

## Maintenance

| Task | Type | Frequency | Cost/Notes |
|------|------|-----------|------------|
| Oil change | Gas | 25–50 hours or annual | $5–$15 |
| Air filter | Gas | Annual or as needed | $5–$20 |
| Spark plug | Gas | Annual | $3–$10 |
| Carburetor cleaning | Gas | As needed (rough idle) | $0 (DIY) / $50–$100 (shop) |
| Fuel stabilizer | Gas | Every storage season | $10/bottle (treats many gallons) |
| Battery storage | Battery | Winter | Store indoors at 40–60% charge |
| Blade/chain sharpening | Both | Annual / as needed | Same procedure gas or battery |
| Battery replacement | Battery | 3–5 years | $80–$200 per battery |

**Gas off-season**: Add fuel stabilizer and run 5 min, or drain fuel. Stale
gas (30+ days) causes carburetor issues — the #1 gas tool failure after
storage. **Battery longevity**: Store at 40–60% charge, room temperature.
Never store discharged (can drop below recovery voltage).

## Noise Ordinances

| Regulation type | Examples |
|----------------|----------|
| Gas blower bans | Many California cities, Washington DC, dozens of municipalities |
| Noise limits | Enforced dB limits during certain hours |
| Seasonal restrictions | Some HOAs limit gas tool use to certain days/hours |

**Check local ordinances before buying gas** — trend is toward electrification.

## Safety

| Gear | When | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Eye protection | All tools | Debris, line fragments, chips |
| Ear protection | Gas tools, blowers | 85+ dB causes hearing damage |
| Chainsaw chaps | Chainsaws | Stops chain on contact — saves legs |
| Steel-toe boots | Chainsaws, mowers | Foot protection |
| Gloves | All tools | Grip, vibration, cuts |

## Storage

| Tool type | Storage requirements |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Gas | Ventilated area (fumes), fuel stabilizer or drain for off-season |
| Battery | Climate-controlled, batteries indoors in winter |
| Corded | Dry area, cord coiled (no kinks) |

## Yard Size Matching

| Yard size | Recommended power source | Notes |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| Under 0.25 acre | Battery or corded | Single battery sufficient |
| 0.25–0.5 acre | Battery with spare, or gas | Second battery needed for mower |
| 0.5–1 acre | Gas or commercial battery platform | EGO/DeWalt 60V can handle this |
| 1+ acre | Gas or commercial battery | Riding mower likely needed |

**Under 0.25 acre**: Battery or corded. **0.5+ acre**: Gas or premium battery
platforms (EGO, DeWalt 60V FlexVolt) with spare batteries.
