# Small Appliances — Domain Reference

Domain-specific constraints for small kitchen appliances: blenders, food
processors, pressure cookers, fryers, stand mixers, and meat grinders.
Referenced by `references/constraint-attributes.md`. Load when the user is
buying a small kitchen appliance. Small appliances span $30–$800+; power,
capacity, and attachments are primary decision drivers; warranty and
repairability determine long-term value.

## Power (Watts)

Higher wattage means better performance for tough tasks (dough, frozen
ingredients, grinding meat). Underpowered appliances stall, overheat, and
burn out motors. **500W minimum for dough, heavy batters, frozen blending,
or meat grinding.** Low-wattage stand mixers (KitchenAid tilt-head ~250–325W)
use gear reduction to deliver torque — compare by motor type, not just watts.

| Appliance | Minimum | Typical | Heavy-duty |
|-----------|---------|---------|------------|
| Blender | 500W | 700–1200W | 1500W+ (Vitamix/Blendtec) |
| Food processor | 400W | 600–900W | 1000W+ |
| Pressure cooker | 700W | 1000–1200W | 1300W+ |
| Air fryer | 800W | 1200–1500W | 1700W+ |
| Stand mixer | 250W (tilt-head) | 500–800W (bowl-lift) | 1000W+ |
| Meat grinder | 300W | 500–1000W | 1500W+ |

## Capacity

Match capacity to household size and typical batch size. Too small = multiple
batches; too large = wasted counter space and harder cleaning.

| Appliance | 1–2 people | 3–4 people | 5+ people |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Blender (oz) | 48 oz | 64 oz | 72+ oz |
| Food processor (cups) | 7 cup | 11–14 cup | 14–16 cup |
| Pressure cooker (qt) | 3–4 qt | 6 qt | 8 qt |
| Air fryer (qt) | 2–4 qt | 5–6 qt | 7–8 qt |
| Stand mixer (qt) | 4.5 qt | 5–6 qt | 7–8 qt |
| Meat grinder (lb/min) | 2–3 | 4–5 | 6+ |

## Attachments

- **Included vs add-on**: Many stand mixers and food processors advertise
  "10+ attachments" but include only 2–3 in the box. Check what's included.
- **Stand mixer attachments can cost more than the mixer**: pasta roller
  ($150), meat grinder ($100), food processor ($150), grain mill ($200).
  Budget for attachments separately.
- **Blender jars**: Some models include a personal cup; others sell it as a
  $30–$50 add-on. Tamper tool (Vitamix) is essential for thick blends.
- **Pressure cooker accessories**: Steamer basket, trivet, sealing rings
  (silicone rings absorb odors — buy spares).

## Warranty & Reliability

| Brand | Typical warranty | Reliability tier |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Vitamix | 5–10 yr (full) | Premium — 15+ yr lifespan common |
| Blendtec | 8 yr (base) | Premium |
| KitchenAid | 1 yr (5 yr motor on some) | Premium — mixers last decades |
| Breville | 1–2 yr | Premium |
| Cuisinart | 1–3 yr | Mid-range |
| Ninja / Instant Pot | 1 yr | Budget — shorter lifespan |

**Motor warranties**: Some brands offer 5+ years on the motor only
(labor/parts may be limited after year 1). Read the fine print.

## Noise

Blenders and food processors are among the loudest kitchen appliances.
High-speed blenders reach 85–95 dB — **hearing damage at sustained 85+**.
Food processors: 80–90 dB. Stand mixers: 65–75 dB. A 90+ dB blender for 2–3
minutes daily is unlikely to cause damage, but prolonged commercial use
requires hearing protection.

## Cleaning & Maintenance

- **Dishwasher-safe parts**: Check which parts are safe — often the jar is,
  but the blade assembly and motor base are not.
- **Sealed vs removable blade**: Sealed blades (some food processors) are
  harder to clean; removable blades allow full access.
- **Self-cleaning**: Some blenders (Vitamix, Blendtec) have a self-clean
  cycle (water + drop of soap, 60 seconds). Convenient but not a substitute
  for periodic deep cleaning.

## Counter Space & Storage

- **Measure the footprint** — stand mixers and high-speed blenders are large
  and heavy (15–25 lb). If it lives in a cabinet, the user won't use it.
- **Cord length**: Typically 24–36 inches. Short cords may not reach outlets.
- **Height clearance**: Stand mixers need 16–18 inches vertical to lift the
  bowl — check upper cabinets.

## Speed & Settings

- **Variable speed** (dial) is more versatile than preset buttons — allows
  precise control for different ingredients.
- **Preset programs**: Convenient for common tasks but less flexible.
- **Pulse function**: Essential for food processors and blenders — burst
  control for chopping without over-processing.

## Used Market

Premium brands (Vitamix, Blendtec, KitchenAid, Breville) hold value and are
good used buys. Budget brands (Ninja, Instant Pot) are often not worth used —
new prices are low and condition is unpredictable. **Used checks**:

- **Blade wear**: Inspect for nicks, dullness, bent tips. Vitamix/Blendtec
  blades are replaceable ($50–$80).
- **Motor bearings**: Run at high speed empty — listen for grinding, squealing,
  or excessive vibration indicating bearing wear.
- **Container/jar condition**: Cracks, cloudiness, or lingering odors in
  plastic indicate heavy use.
- **Warranty transfer**: Vitamix warranties are tied to the machine, not the
  owner — verify serial number. Blendtec/KitchenAid may require original
  receipt.
- **Refurbished**: Manufacturer-refurbished units (Vitamix Certified
  Reconditioned) carry full warranties at 30–40% off — often better value
  than third-party used.
