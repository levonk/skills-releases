# Constraint Identification Checklist — Reference

Detailed constraint research checklist for products and services. Referenced by `SKILL.md` section "5. Constraint Identification".

## Product Constraints

- **Product defects**: Known recalls (check [NHTSA](https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls), [CPSC](https://www.cpsc.gov/Recalls)), firmware bugs, class-action lawsuits
- **Version pitfalls**: "Never buy Model X revision 2; revision 3 fixed the motherboard issue"
- **Reliability data**: Mean time to failure, warranty claim rates, long-term review aggregation
- **Seller reputation**: BBB rating, Trustpilot score, return policy quality
- **Environmental hazards**: "Hurricane season starts June 1 — outdoor installation should complete before then", "Tornado alley peak is April–June"
- **Buy-new vs buy-used rules**: Items that should never be bought used (car seats, helmets, mattresses, tires) vs items that are fine used (cast iron, hand tools, furniture)
- **Mileage / wear thresholds**: For vehicles — avoid over 100k miles on CVT transmissions; for appliances — avoid units past warranty with known compressor issues

## Service Constraints

- **Licensing**: Does the jurisdiction require a license for this work? (plumbing, electrical, HVAC, roofing, tree removal in many states)
- **Insurance & bonding**: Is the provider insured? Will they provide a certificate of insurance? Are they bonded?
- **Permits**: Does the work require a building permit? Who pulls it — homeowner or contractor?
- **Warranty/guarantee**: Does the provider offer a workmanship warranty? How long? What does it cover?
- **Complaint history**: Check BBB, state contractor licensing board, Yelp filtered reviews
- **Seasonal availability**: Roofers are booked solid after hail season; HVAC techs are slammed in summer/winter peaks
- **Red flags**: Demands full payment upfront, no written estimate, no physical address, pressure to decide immediately, won't provide references
